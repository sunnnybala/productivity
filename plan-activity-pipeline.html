My Activity Tracking Plan

Plain-English version. The goal: see exactly where my time goes, on my laptop and my phone, all in one place.

What I'm building (in one sentence):

A system that quietly collects what I do on my laptop (already happening via ActivityWatch) and on my phone (app usage), and sends both into one shared online database so I can analyze my habits later. Built so my AI agent can summarize it for me down the road, without rebuilding anything.

The big picture

Three places, one destination. My laptop and phone each send their activity to a cloud database called Supabase. That's the whole of Phase 1.

   LAPTOP                                  THE CLOUD DATABASE (Supabase)
   ActivityWatch  ──(sends once a day)──▶  ┌────────────────────────┐
                                           │  laptop_events         │
                                           │  phone_app_usage       │
   PHONE                                   │  (+ a summary table     │
   tiny app  ─────(sends once a day)────▶  │   ready for later)      │
                                           └────────────────────────┘
                                                      │
                                            LATER (Phase 2): my AI agent
                                            reads this, writes a daily
                                            summary into my "brain" (gbrain)
Why this design is safe:

The collecting half only ever writes the raw data. The summarizing half (later) only reads it. They never interfere. That's why I can build the collecting part now and add the smart summaries later with zero rework.

The 4 simple pieces

1
A cloud database (Supabase) A free online database that both devices send data to. It has separate "drawers" for laptop data and phone data, because they look very different.
2
A small laptop script Runs once a day, grabs everything ActivityWatch recorded since last time, and uploads it. Written in PowerShell (already built into Windows, no extra installs).
3
A tiny phone app A small custom app I'll build for you. Once a day it reads your phone's app-usage (the same numbers Digital Wellbeing shows) and uploads them. Set it up once, then forget it.
4
(Later) The AI summary Phase 2, not now. Your agent reads the database each day and writes a short, useful summary into your brain. We've shaped everything so this just plugs in later.

The decisions we locked in

QuestionWhat we choseWhy, simply
How to store the dataTwo separate tablesLaptop = thousands of tiny events/day. Phone = a few daily totals. Different shapes, so keep them apart and tidy.
How often to uploadOnce a day (when you next use the device)Barely touches your battery, and it can never lose a day (explained below).
Laptop script languagePowerShellAlready on Windows. Nothing extra to install.
Phone methodCustom appReliable and low-maintenance. Won't silently break like no-code tools can.

"But what if a day gets missed?" — it can't

This was your main worry, and the design solves it for free:

  • The laptop script remembers where it left off.
  • ActivityWatch keeps your full history on the laptop and never deletes it.
  • So whenever the script runs, it uploads everything since last time — even if that's a week.

Uploading less often only makes the data a bit older, never missing. No permanent gaps, ever.
